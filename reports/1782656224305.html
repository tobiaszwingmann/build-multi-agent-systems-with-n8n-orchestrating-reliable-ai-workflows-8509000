Consolidated Research Findings: Walt Disney Co. (DIS) Earnings Reviews (FY25–FY26)

Executive Summary

Across the consolidated earnings reviews for Walt Disney Co. (DIS) spanning Q1 FY25 through Q2 FY26, the research notes consistently express an Outperform rating. The notes cite a recurring set of positives, including direct-to-consumer (DTC) profitability, studio rebound, and durable profitability from Experiences, with Sports improvements mentioned in the Q3 FY25 note. Later updates also highlight a broader earnings/EPS growth framework and an accelerating capital return program alongside streaming profitability and ongoing Experiences economics. Overall, the ratings and price targets are presented as increasing or remaining constructive over time, reflecting a sustained positive view in the covered notes.

Original Research Question / Hypothesis

Whether Walt Disney Co. (DIS) merits an “Outperform” stance based on developments highlighted in each quarterly earnings review (e.g., DTC profitability, studio performance, Experiences/Sports performance, and longer-term earnings framework).

Key Findings

  1. The research notes maintain an “Outperform” rating throughout all provided quarters (Q1 FY25 to Q2 FY26).
  • Q1 FY25: The note explicitly states “We rate DIS Outperform” and provides a “$115 12-month price target.” (Source: documents/disneyq1fy25researchnote.pdf, Feb 5, 2025)
  • Q2 FY25: The note is again labeled “Outperform.” (Source: documents/disneyq2fy25researchnote.pdf)
  • Q3 FY25: The note explicitly states “We rate DIS Outperform” with an “illustrative $125 12-month price target.” (Source: documents/disneyq3fy25researchnote.pdf, Aug 6, 2025)
  • Q4 FY25: The note reiterates “We rate DIS Outperform” with an “illustrative $135 12-month price target.” (Source: documents/disneyq4fy25researchnote.pdf, Nov 13, 2025)
  • Q2 FY26: The note again states “We rate DIS Outperform” with a “$125 12-month price target.” (Source: documents/disneyq2fy26researchnote.pdf, May 6, 2026)
  1. Core drivers cited across multiple quarters include DTC profitability and durable Experiences economics.
  • Q1 FY25: Rationale includes “DTC profitability, studio rebound, and durable Experiences profitability.” (Source: documents/disneyq1fy25researchnote.pdf, Feb 5, 2025)
  • Q3 FY25: Rationale includes “DTC profits scaled” and “Experiences remained durable.” (Source: documents/disneyq3fy25researchnote.pdf, Aug 6, 2025)
  • Q2 FY26: Rationale includes “Streaming profitability” and “durable Experiences economics.” (Source: documents/disneyq2fy26researchnote.pdf, May 6, 2026)
  1. The notes also reference studio performance improvements and, in one quarter, Sports improvement.
  • Q1 FY25: Studio rebound is explicitly cited in the rationale (“studio rebound”). (Source: documents/disneyq1fy25researchnote.pdf, Feb 5, 2025)
  • Q3 FY25: Rationale includes “Sports improved” (as part of the broader list of factors). (Source: documents/disneyq3fy25researchnote.pdf, Aug 6, 2025)
  1. Price targets presented in the notes trend higher into Q4 FY25, followed by a reset/leveling in Q2 FY26 based on the note’s framing.
  • Q1 FY25: “$115 12-month price target.” (Source: documents/disneyq1fy25researchnote.pdf, Feb 5, 2025)
  • Q3 FY25: “illustrative $125 12-month price target.” (Source: documents/disneyq3fy25researchnote.pdf, Aug 6, 2025)
  • Q4 FY25: “illustrative $135 12-month price target.” (Source: documents/disneyq4fy25researchnote.pdf, Nov 13, 2025)
  • Q2 FY26: “$125 12-month price target.” (Source: documents/disneyq2fy26researchnote.pdf, May 6, 2026)
  1. Later-quarter commentary emphasizes an earnings framework and capital return actions alongside streaming profitability and Experiences.
  • Q4 FY25: Rationale references “FY26 and FY27 EPS growth framework… support an Outperform rating.” (Source: documents/disneyq4fy25researchnote.pdf, Nov 13, 2025)
  • Q2 FY26: Rationale includes an “accelerating capital return program” alongside “Streaming profitability” and “durable Experiences economics.” (Source: documents/disneyq2fy26researchnote.pdf, May 6, 2026)
