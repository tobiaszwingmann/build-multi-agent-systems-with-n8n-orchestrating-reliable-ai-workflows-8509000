# Disney (DIS) — Internal Assessment Through 2025

## Executive summary
This report consolidates internal research notes on The Walt Disney Company (DIS) across 2025. The internal view remained an Outperform rating throughout the year while conviction shifted from a constructive turnaround to a higher‑confidence multi‑year earnings and free‑cash‑flow (FCF) growth thesis supported by distribution and capital‑returns actions. Key catalysts included demonstrable progress toward direct‑to‑consumer (DTC) profitability, resilient Experiences performance, ESPN DTC initiatives, and a late‑year buyback/dividend program that supported valuation. Persistent risks cited by the research team included macro/consumer weakness, sports rights inflation, streaming churn/ARPU uncertainty, parks cyclicality, and execution/timing risks.

## Original research question / hypothesis
Can Disney transition from a turnaround story to a sustained multi‑year earnings and FCF growth company driven by DTC profitability and resilient Experiences, justifying an Outperform rating and increasing price targets through 2025? (Source: internal Q2/Q3/Q4 FY25 notes.)

## Key findings
- Rating and conviction path
  - The internal rating remained Outperform throughout 2025. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Q3 FY25 note; Q4 FY25 note]
  - Conviction progressed from a "constructive turnaround" view after Q2 to stronger certainty in DTC profitability and durable Experiences after Q3, and finally to a multi‑year EPS/FCF growth plus shareholder returns thesis by Q4. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note; Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]

- Drivers of increasing confidence (each listed driver attributed to internal notes)
  - Better‑than‑expected quarterly results and raised guidance (Q2 beat/raise; management raised FY25 adj. EPS guidance to $5.85). [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note]
  - Clear progress toward DTC operating profitability and visible margin expansion. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note; Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]
  - Resilient Experiences (parks/cruises) performance and record Experiences profit in Q4. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]
  - ESPN DTC initiatives and distribution deals viewed as catalysts for future growth. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note; Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]
  - Star India deconsolidation / joint‑venture benefits noted as a positive structural item. [Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note]
  - Capital returns (authorization of $7bn buyback and a $1.50 dividend) cited as valuation support in Q4. [Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]

- Price target (PT) evolution and valuation basis
  - Post‑Q2 FY25: PT = $120, implied ~19x FY26E EPS of ~$6.30 (~21% upside noted at that time). [Post‑Q2 FY25 note]
  - Aug. 6, 2025 (Q3 FY25): PT raised to $125, based on ~19x FY26E EPS of ~$6.55. [Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note]
  - Nov. 13, 2025 (Q4 FY25): PT raised to $135, based on ~19x FY27E EPS of ~$7.10. [Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]

- Net evolution over 2025 (summary claims with citations)
  - The internal view moved from a constructive turnaround to higher‑conviction earnings recovery and by year‑end to a multi‑year EPS/FCF growth story with capital returns. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note; Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]
  - The research team identified DTC profitability visibility, resilient Experiences, ESPN DTC/distribution catalysts, Star India JV benefits, and buybacks/dividend as the principal contributors to increased confidence. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note; Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]

- Persisting risks called out by the research notes
  - Macro/consumer weakness and parks cyclicality. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note]
  - Sports rights inflation and streaming churn/ARPU uncertainty. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note]
  - Studio slate volatility, capex/tax/timing risk, and execution risk around parks/capex. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]

## Conclusion
Across 2025 the internal research team maintained an Outperform rating on Disney while steadily increasing price targets from $120 to $135 as confidence grew that DTC operating profitability combined with durable Experiences and capital returns would support multi‑year EPS and FCF growth. The team nevertheless continued to flag macro, content, sports rights, streaming ARPU/churn, and operational risks that could impair the thesis. [Post‑Q2 FY25 note; Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 note; Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 note]
