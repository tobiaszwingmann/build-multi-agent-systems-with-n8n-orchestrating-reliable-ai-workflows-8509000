# Short Title
Internal Assessment: Disney — End-2025 vs. Post‑Q1 FY26 Public Events

## Executive summary
During 2025 our internal view of Disney remained consistently constructive: an Outperform rating each quarter, rising 12‑month targets (≈ $115 → $135), and progressive EPS upgrades driven by streaming margin inflection, studio recovery and strong Parks cash flow (documents/disney_q1_fy25_research_note.pdf; documents/disney_q2_fy25_research_note.pdf; documents/disney_q3_fy25_research_note.pdf; documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf). As of Q1 FY2026 (quarter ended Dec. 27, 2025) there is no internal “Q1/2026” investment note in the repo; the most recent internal view in the requested window is the Q4 FY25 note (Nov 13, 2025) (file list from repo; documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf). Public events around Q1 FY26 (Feb 2–3, 2026) — notably Disney stopping quarterly paid‑subscriber disclosures, parks/international visitation headwinds, and CEO succession — introduced new uncertainty and shifted the evidence base from subscriber volumes to profitability and leadership execution (SEC filing; press release Feb 2, 2026; CEO succession announcement Feb 3, 2026). Based on the available internal notes plus those public developments, the constructive thesis (Outperform) likely remained intact near Q1 FY26 but with more caveats and closer monitoring of parks, carriage outcomes, and execution under new leadership (documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf; press release Feb 2, 2026).

## Original research question / hypothesis
How did the firm's internal investment stance on Disney change between the end‑of‑2025 internal view (Q4 FY25 note, Nov 13, 2025) and the period immediately following Q1 FY26 public events (Jan–Mar 31, 2026)?

## Key findings
- Internal stance through 2025: consistently constructive with an Outperform rating and rising price targets each quarter (Q1 FY25 ≈ $115 → Q2 FY25 ≈ $120 → Q3 FY25 ≈ $125 → Q4 FY25 ≈ $135) and progressive near‑term EPS upgrades tied to streaming margin improvement and Parks recovery (documents/disney_q1_fy25_research_note.pdf; documents/disney_q2_fy25_research_note.pdf; documents/disney_q3_fy25_research_note.pdf; documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf).
- Specific quarter summaries (internal notes):
  - Q1 FY25 (covers results Feb 5, 2025): constructive tone; Outperform; 12‑month target ≈ $115; modeled FY25 adj. EPS ≈ $5.39 → FY26E adj. EPS ≈ $5.99; drivers: early streaming stabilization and parks recovery; action: maintain constructive stance (documents/disney_q1_fy25_research_note.pdf).
  - Q2 FY25 (mid‑2025 earnings review): constructive tone; target ≈ $120; management raised FY25 adj. EPS guidance; FY26E adj. EPS ≈ $6.30 used in modeling; drivers: improving DTC economics and parks momentum (documents/disney_q2_fy25_research_note.pdf).
  - Q3 FY25 (Aug 6, 2025): constructive tone; target ≈ $125; FY26E adj. EPS ≈ $6.55; highlights streaming margin inflection and Experiences strength (documents/disney_q3_fy25_research_note.pdf).
  - Q4 FY25 (Nov 13, 2025): constructive/positive tone; Outperform; 12‑month target ≈ $135; FY25 adj. EPS reported ≈ $5.93; FY27E adj. EPS used in valuation (~$7.10) with implied multiples ≈ ~19x; drivers: DTC profitability inflection, record Parks cash generation, studio tailwinds, improving FCF enabling capital returns; action: continue Outperform and monitor ESPN rights timing, capex/cruise spend, and DTC margin progress (documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf).
- Net change through 2025: sequential EPS upgrades and higher price targets each quarter as results/guidance and streaming margin progress outperformed prior internal assumptions; consistent buy case centered on streaming margin inflection plus durable Parks cash flow; persistent monitoring items included carriage/distribution risk, ESPN rights/costs, India/JioStar comparability, and capex timing for parks/cruise (documents/disney_q1_fy25_research_note.pdf; documents/disney_q2_fy25_research_note.pdf; documents/disney_q3_fy25_research_note.pdf; documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf).
- Repo status and Q1 FY26 documents: no internal Q1 FY26 investment note or file was located in the repo for Jan 1–Mar 31, 2026; the Q4 FY25 note (Nov 13, 2025) is the last internal position in the requested window (file list from repo; documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf).
- Public developments after Q4 FY25 that materially affected inputs:
  - June 28, 2025 (filed earlier but relevant to Q1 FY26 reporting): Disney stopped publicly reporting quarterly paid‑subscriber counts/ARPU, reducing visibility into growth/churn and shifting reliance to OI/margins (SEC filing).
  - Oct–Nov 2025: YouTube TV carriage dispute and Oct 29 closing of Hulu+LiveTV/Fubo deal introduced near‑term distribution/affiliate revenue noise referenced in internal commentary (documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf).
  - Feb 2, 2026 (Q1 FY26 release): company highlighted SVOD operating income improvement but flagged international visitation headwinds in Parks; last public subscriber totals remained those reported as of Sept. 27, 2025 (press release Feb 2, 2026).
  - Feb 3, 2026: CEO succession announced (Josh D’Amaro named CEO; Dana Walden President & CCO), prompting reassessment of strategy and execution risk (CEO succession announcement Feb 3, 2026).
- Practical impacts on internal assessment vs end‑of‑2025:
  - Visibility/metrics: a shift from subscriber counts to profitability metrics as primary streaming inputs increased model uncertainty for growth assumptions while reducing reliance on a single public KPI (SEC filing; press release Feb 2, 2026).
  - Risk profile: parks international visitation weakness and carriage noise introduced near‑term revenue downside risk even as streaming OI improvements validated parts of the buy case (press release Feb 2, 2026; documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf).
  - Governance/strategy: CEO succession likely shifted emphasis toward Parks/Experiences (D’Amaro background) and invited re‑forecasting of content/streaming strategy and capital allocation (CEO succession announcement Feb 3, 2026).
  - Overall stance: combining internal notes through Nov 13, 2025 and public developments through early Feb 2026, the constructive thesis (Outperform) likely remained intact near Q1 FY26 but with more caveats and closer monitoring of parks visitation trends, carriage outcomes, and execution under new leadership; markets priced some of these concerns (share drops on Nov 13, 2025 and Feb 2–3, 2026) (documents/disney_q4_fy25_research_note.pdf; press release Feb 2, 2026).
