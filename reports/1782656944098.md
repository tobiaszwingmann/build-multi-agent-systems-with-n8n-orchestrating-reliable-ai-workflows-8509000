## Disney Q1 FY26 Results: Streaming Profitability Rise, Subscriber Metrics De-emphasized, and Tepid Forward Tone

### Executive Summary
Disney’s Q1 FY26 showed revenue growth to $26.0 billion and a substantial year-over-year increase in SVOD operating income, indicating continued improvement in streaming profitability. However, quarter metrics such as diluted EPS and adjusted EPS declined versus Q1 FY25, and total segment operating income fell to $4.6 billion. Notably, Disney did not provide quarter-end paid-subscriber counts in Q1 FY26 materials, instead emphasizing financial SVOD metrics and removing prior detailed subscriber tables. External reporting around the release characterized the outlook/guidance tone as tepid and linked market weakness to concerns including international park visitation and the shift in subscriber disclosure emphasis.

---

## Original Research Question / Hypothesis
**How did Disney’s Q1 FY26 performance (financial results, SVOD profitability, and guidance) compare with the firm’s earlier Q1 FY25 assessment, and what changes in disclosures or market interpretation affected investor takeaway?**

---

## Key Findings

1. **Revenue increased in Q1 FY26, while some earnings metrics declined year-over-year.**  
   Disney reported **Q1 FY26 revenue of $26.0B** compared with **$24.7B in Q1 FY25**. However, **diluted EPS fell to $1.34 (from $1.40)** and **adjusted EPS fell to $1.63 (from $1.76)**.  
   *Sources:* Disney Q1 FY26 earnings release (Feb 2, 2026) https://investors.thewaltdisneycompany.com/news/news-details/2026/The-Walt-Disney-Company-Reports-First-Quarter-Earnings-for-Fiscal-2026/default.aspx ; Disney Q1 FY25 SEC exhibit/press release (Feb 5, 2025) https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1744489/000174448925000066/fy2025_q1xprxex991.htm ; Round 2 synthesis (internal findings)

2. **Total segment operating income declined in Q1 FY26 versus Q1 FY25, contrasting with the prior-quarter “broadening” view.**  
   Disney reported **total segment operating income of $4.6B in Q1 FY26** versus **$5.1B in Q1 FY25**. This was noted as a divergence from the firm’s earlier internal view that operating profitability was broadening after the FY25 inflection.  
   *Sources:* Disney Q1 FY26 earnings release (Feb 2, 2026) link above ; Disney Q1 FY25 SEC exhibit/press release (Feb 5, 2025) link above ; Internal research note “disney_q1_fy25_research_note.pdf” (Feb 5, 2025) (internal file) ; Round 2 synthesis (internal findings)

3. **SVOD profitability improved sharply year-over-year, with Disney emphasizing financial SVOD metrics.**  
   For Q1 FY26, Disney reported **SVOD revenue of $5,346M** (up from **$4,816M**) and **SVOD operating income of $450M** (up from **$261M**), with management reporting an SVOD operating margin of about **8.4%**. Management attributed SVOD gains to **pricing, subscriber growth in NA and International, and bundling** (including Duo/Trio/Max), along with **integrated Disney+/Hulu** reducing churn and improving profitability.  
   *Sources:* Disney Q1 FY26 earnings release supplemental SVOD table and related reporting (Feb 2, 2026) https://investors.thewaltdisneycompany.com/news/news-details/2026/The-Walt-Disney-Company-Reports-First-Quarter-Earnings-for-Fiscal-2026/default.aspx ; Disney Q1 FY26 Form 10-Q PDF (quarter ended Dec 27, 2025) https://investors.thewaltdisneycompany.com/files/doc_financials/2026/q1/6a458218-80bc-4c84-a57a-768a278eddf8.pdf ; Disney Q1 FY26 earnings call transcript and executive commentary (Feb 2, 2026) https://investors.thewaltdisneycompany.com/files/doc_events/2026/Feb/02/q1-fy26-earnings-transcript.pdf and https://investors.thewaltdisneycompany.com/files/doc_events/2026/Feb/02/Q1-FY26-Executive-Commentary.pdf ; Round 2 synthesis (internal findings) ; Internal Q1 FY25 note: DTC profit inflection framing (internal file)

4. **Disney did not disclose quarter-end paid-subscriber counts in Q1 FY26, shifting transparency toward profitability metrics.**  
   Disney’s Q1 FY26 earnings materials **did not include quarter-end paid-subscriber counts**; instead, Disney reported **SVOD revenue and operating income** and removed the detailed subscriber tables that had been present previously. By contrast, the firm’s baseline Q1 FY25 materials included disclosed subscriber counts (e.g., Disney+ figures cited in the internal baseline summary).  
   *Sources:* Disney Q1 FY26 earnings release and Q1 FY26 Form 10-Q (Feb 2, 2026) and (Feb 2026 PDF) links above ; Disney Q1 FY25 SEC exhibit/press release (Feb 5, 2025) link above ; Round 2 synthesis (internal findings)

5. **Disney previously justified moving away from routine subscriber/ARPU tables in favor of DTC financial metrics.**  
   Disney’s **Q3 FY25 executive commentary** states management believed the recurring quarter-end paid-subscriber and ARPU tables had become **“less meaningful”** due to changes in DTC strategy (bundling, integrated experiences), and that the company would emphasize DTC financial metrics (revenue, operating income) rather than routine subscriber tables.  
   *Sources:* Disney Q3 FY25 Executive Commentary (Aug 2025) https://thewaltdisneycompany.com/app/uploads/2025/08/q3-fy25-executive-commentary.pdf?utm_source=openai ; Round 3 finding (external rationale)

6. **Guidance and targets were more assertive for FY26 streaming profitability and EPS than FY25’s frame used in the firm’s prior note.**  
   In Q1 FY26 disclosures, Disney guided for **Entertainment double-digit segment operating income growth**, a **SVOD operating margin target of ~10%**, and **double-digit adjusted EPS growth** for FY26, along with **$19B cash provided by operations** and being **on track to repurchase $7B of stock**. This was assessed as more ambitious than the firm’s Q1 FY25 cited guidance context (e.g., **high-single-digit adjusted EPS growth** and **~$15B operating cash flow**).  
   *Sources:* Disney Q1 FY26 earnings release “Guidance and Outlook” (Feb 2, 2026) link above ; Disney Q1 FY25 release (Feb 5, 2025) link above ; Internal research note “disney_q1_fy25_research_note.pdf” (Feb 5, 2025) (internal file) ; Round 2 synthesis (internal findings)

7. **External coverage characterized the release as a headline beat with a tepid forward tone and related stock weakness.**  
   Bloomberg reported that despite top-line strength, investors perceived the forward outlook/guidance tone as **tepid**, with shares falling on guidance and **park/international visitor** concerns. Reuters (via Investing.com) similarly noted a **~5%** share decline, citing weaker commentary on international park visitation and mixed TV/film results alongside pretax income beating expectations.  
   *Sources:* Bloomberg (Feb 2, 2026) https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-02/disney-beats-forecasts-with-record-park-sales-ahead-of-ceo-pick?utm_source=openai ; Reuters / Investing.com (Feb 2, 2026) https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/disney-theme-parks-help-boost-earnings-above-wall-street-forecasts-4478388?utm_source=openai ; Round 3 finding (external reactions and market move summary)

8. **The firm’s internal repository found no explicit internal reaction to the decision to stop disclosing quarter-end paid-subscriber counts.**  
   Round 3 internal repository search reported a **gap**: no internal file was located that explicitly discussed an internal reaction to the change in subscriber disclosure.  
   *Sources:* Round 3 internal repository negative search result (internal findings)

---

## Conclusion
Overall, Disney’s Q1 FY26 results supported a key positive narrative around **streaming profitability improvement** (higher SVOD revenue and materially higher SVOD operating income). At the same time, the quarter showed **declines in EPS and total segment operating income year-over-year**, and Disney **changed disclosure practice** by not publishing quarter-end paid-subscriber counts. External media coverage linked the market reaction to **perceived tepid guidance tone** and concerns such as **international park visitation**, with additional commentary around the emphasis on profitability rather than subscriber counts.
