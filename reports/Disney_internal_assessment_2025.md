# Internal Assessment: The Walt Disney Company (DIS) — 2025

## Executive summary
The firm maintained an Outperform rating on Disney throughout 2025 while its internal view evolved from a constructive turnaround to a higher-conviction multi-year EPS/FCF growth story with shareholder returns. Progress toward DTC operating profitability, resilient Experiences performance, and improving cash flow underpinned rising conviction and successive price-target increases. Management guidance raises, distribution/ESPN DTC catalysts, and a late-year buyback/dividend program were highlighted as catalysts supporting the upgraded view. Persistent risks remained macro/consumer weakness, parks cyclicality, sports rights inflation, streaming churn/ARPU volatility, studio slate risk, and capex/timing uncertainty (Internal Q2 FY25; Internal Q3 FY25; Internal Q4 FY25).

## Original research question / hypothesis
Can Disney’s direct-to-consumer (DTC) initiatives reach operating profitability while Experiences remain resilient, such that the company transitions from a turnaround story to a sustained multi-year EPS and free-cash-flow growth trajectory that supports valuation upside and shareholder returns? (Internal Q2 FY25; Internal Q3 FY25; Internal Q4 FY25)

## Key findings

- Post-Q2 FY25 assessment
  - Rating: Outperform; internal view described as a constructive turnaround. DTC/streaming was viewed as moving toward profitability and Experiences were characterized as resilient. The Q2 beat/raise, improving ARPU, ESPN DTC initiatives, and free-cash-flow generation were cited as supporting the thesis. Persistent risks included macro pressure, sports rights inflation, churn/ARPU uncertainty, and parks/capex execution. Price target: $120 (~19x FY26E EPS of ~$6.30; ~21% upside). (Internal Q2 FY25)

- Aug. 6, 2025 — Q3 FY25 assessment
  - Rating: Outperform; confidence increased. Management raised FY25 adjusted EPS guidance to $5.85 and DTC profitability became more visible, prompting a shift from “turnaround potential” to stronger conviction that DTC profitability plus durable Experiences would drive multi-year EPS growth. Key catalysts noted included distribution deals, ESPN DTC launch plans, Star India JV benefits, and cruise/parks expansion. Price target raised to $125 (~19x FY26E EPS of ~$6.55). (Internal Q3 FY25)

- Nov. 13, 2025 — Q4 FY25 assessment
  - Rating: Outperform; conviction further increased. The thesis broadened from an earnings recovery to sustained EPS growth plus shareholder returns. Drivers emphasized were DTC margin expansion, record Experiences profit, stronger cash flow, ESPN DTC progress, and capital return actions. Management authorized a $7 billion buyback and announced a $1.50 dividend. Price target raised to $135 (~19x FY27E EPS of ~$7.10). (Internal Q4 FY25)

- Net evolution during 2025
  - The Outperform rating was maintained throughout 2025. The internal view progressed from a constructive turnaround to higher-conviction earnings recovery and ultimately to a multi-year EPS/FCF growth story with capital returns. The price-target path over the year was $120 → $125 → $135. (Internal Q2 FY25; Internal Q3 FY25; Internal Q4 FY25)

- Principal drivers of increased conviction (as cited across the notes)
  - Better-than-expected quarterly results and raised guidance (Internal Q2 FY25; Internal Q3 FY25)
  - Clear progress in DTC operating profitability and visible margin expansion (Internal Q2 FY25; Internal Q3 FY25; Internal Q4 FY25)
  - Resilient Experiences (record profit called out in Q4) (Internal Q2 FY25; Internal Q4 FY25)
  - ESPN DTC progress and distribution/launch catalysts (Internal Q3 FY25; Internal Q4 FY25)
  - Star India deconsolidation/JV benefits called out as a positive catalyst (Internal Q3 FY25)
  - Capital return actions (authorised $7bn buyback and $1.50 dividend) providing valuation support (Internal Q4 FY25)

- Persistent risks (noted across the year)
  - Macro/consumer weakness, parks cyclicality, sports-rights inflation, streaming churn/ARPU risk, studio-slate volatility, and capex/tax/timing risk remained cited as ongoing risks to the thesis. (Internal Q2 FY25; Internal Q3 FY25; Internal Q4 FY25)
