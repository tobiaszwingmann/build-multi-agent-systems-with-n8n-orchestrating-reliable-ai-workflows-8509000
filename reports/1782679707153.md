# Disney: From Recovery to Compounding

## Executive Summary
Our internal view on Disney became steadily more bullish through 2025, moving from a turnaround/recovery story to a compounding earnings story. Early 2025 emphasis centered on DTC profitability, studio rebound, Sports recovery, and resilient Experiences, with some caution around free cash flow and capex. By mid-2025, the thesis strengthened as DTC profits appeared more repeatable and Experiences remained durable, while late 2025 focused on FY26 double-digit EPS growth, buybacks, dividend support, and record Experiences. Compared with Q1/2026, the view was even more positive, highlighting SVOD margin expansion, revenue-led beats, EPS compounding into FY27, and continued strength in Experiences and buybacks.

## Original Research Question / Hypothesis
Whether Disney’s business mix was evolving from a recovery/turnaround setup into a durable compounding earnings story, supported by streaming profitability and strength in Experiences. [Source: Consolidated Findings]

## Key Findings
- **Early 2025:** Disney was viewed as a **turnaround/recovery** story, driven by DTC profitability, a studio rebound, Sports recovery, and resilient Experiences, with some caution on free cash flow and capex. [Source: Consolidated Findings]
- **Mid-2025:** The thesis strengthened as DTC profits looked more repeatable and Experiences stayed durable; the view was constructive on FY25 guidance and ESPN DTC. [Source: Consolidated Findings]
- **Late 2025:** The story had shifted to **compounding**, supported by FY26 double-digit EPS growth, buybacks, dividends, and record Experiences. [Source: Consolidated Findings]
- **Compared with Q1/2026:** The assessment was even more positive, emphasizing SVOD margin expansion, revenue-led beats, EPS compounding into FY27, and continued strength in Experiences and buybacks. [Source: Consolidated Findings]
- **Bottom line:** 2025 showed a progression from **recovery to compounding**, and by Q1/2026 the internal assessment was more confident that Disney’s streaming and Experiences businesses were supporting a durable earnings growth story. [Source: Consolidated Findings]
